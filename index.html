How to Use This Tool
Step-by-step guide · Click to expand
▼
Part 1 — Setup & Schedule
1
Upload your Joiners Report
Drag and drop your .xlsx Joiners Report onto the upload zone in the sidebar, or click to browse. The app reads the PLANNED sheet automatically and filters for New Hire status only.
💡 You can re-upload a new file at any time — it replaces the current list and rebuilds the schedule instantly.
2
Fill in your name & email
Enter your name and work email in Step 2 — Your Details. This appears as the organizer on every calendar invite, so the joiner sees it coming from you personally — not a generic account.
3
Configure the schedule
Set your daily working hours (e.g. 09:00–17:00) and the assessment window (how many days before embark the meeting should be held). The app automatically assigns each New Hire a 15-minute slot within their window, spilling over to the next day if a day is full.
⏱ 15-min slots
📅 Auto-continues next day
📋 ≤30 days before embark
4
Block dates or time ranges (optional)
Use Block Day to skip a full day (public holiday, weekend, unavailability), or Block Time Range to exclude a window within a day (e.g. 12:00–13:00 for lunch). Blocked slots are automatically skipped and the affected meetings move to the next available slot.
🚫 You can also block a day directly by clicking it on the calendar, then clicking Block Day in the day panel.
Part 2 — Send Teams Meeting Invites
1
Click "Send All"
Click 📅 Send All in the top panel. The app creates one individual Teams meeting per joiner via your Microsoft 365 account — each with a unique Teams link and auto-record enabled.
Tip: Click any day on the calendar first, then use Send Selected Day to send just that day's meetings.
2
Invites send from your account
Each meeting is created on your Microsoft 365 calendar and the invite is sent from your email address. The joiner receives a standard Teams meeting invite they can accept or add to their calendar.
3
Each joiner gets their own private Teams link
Unlike a shared room link, every invite contains a unique Teams meeting URL. Only that joiner and the organizer are in the meeting — fully private and separate from other sessions.
4
Auto-record starts automatically
Each meeting is created with auto-record enabled. Recording starts the moment the meeting begins — no manual action needed. Requires your Microsoft 365 Teams policy to allow cloud recording.
📌 If auto-record doesn't trigger, ask your IT admin to enable Allow cloud recording in Teams meeting policies.
📥
.ics download — fallback option
If the Teams connector is unavailable, use 📥 Download .ics as a fallback. Open the file in Outlook, click Import, and Outlook will send the invites from your account instead.
Part 3 — Manual Invites
✍️
For individual or ad-hoc assessments
Use ✍️ Manual Invite to send a single Teams meeting to anyone — whether they're in the joiner list or not. Two options:
Pick from Joiner List — search and select any New Hire from the uploaded file, set a custom date and time, send their individual Teams invite.
Enter Manually — type any name, email, position and ship. Useful for re-assessments, walk-ins, or candidates not in the current file.
You can also click 📅 Send on any individual slot row in the day panel to send just that one meeting.
Quick FAQ
Does this need a Microsoft 365 connector?
Yes — the app uses the Microsoft 365 connector to create real Teams meetings directly from your calendar. Make sure you're signed into Claude.ai with your CTI Microsoft 365 account. A .ics fallback is available if the connector is unavailable.
Can multiple staff use this tool?
Yes. Each staff member opens the app, fills in their own name and email in Step 2, and sends invites. The meetings are created on their own calendar and the invites come from their account.
What if a joiner doesn't have an email in the file?
They appear flagged in amber in the joiner list. Use Manual Invite → Enter Manually to create their invite once you have their email.
What if too many joiners can't fit in the time window?
The app continues to the next available day automatically. Check the calendar — meetings will spread across multiple days. Extend the window or adjust working hours if needed.
